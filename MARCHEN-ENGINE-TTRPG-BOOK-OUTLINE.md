# The Märchen Engine — TTRPG Core Book Outline

**Date:** 2026-05-28  
**Owner:** Ted Tschopp (Product Owner Decision)  
**Status:** Publication Blueprint (Ready to Draft)

---

## Purpose & Scope

This outline defines the **canonical core book** for The Märchen Engine TTRPG. It chains reading order, clarifies core vs. supplement, and identifies editorial risks. The core book establishes a **complete, playable system** with character creation, resolution, three major subsystems (combat, social engagement, magic), and one integrated campaign example. Subsequent supplements build on this foundation.

---

## Organizing Principles

1. **Complete System, Not Fragments:** Every chapter must be self-sufficient enough for table play. No reader should finish the book and ask, "How do I actually start?"
2. **Layered Complexity:** Introduce mechanics at their point of use. Core resolution (4D8) comes early; advanced modifiers and subsystem interactions come later.
3. **Narrative Scaffolding:** Each system explains *why* it exists (flavor) before *how* it works (mechanics). The Märchen Engine treats morality, magic, and social interaction as story-worthy, not optimization hooks.
4. **Accessibility & Inclusivity:** Maintain the published intro's tone: welcoming, human-centered, joyful play over win-states. "Stay in Communication" is a rule, not flavor text.

## Part Structure & Chapters

### **PART I: FOUNDATIONS (The Welcoming)**

**Reading Goal:** Establish tone, introduce core concepts, make character creation possible.

#### **Chapter 1: Welcome to the Märchen Engine**
- *Source:* Published intro post (2023-09-30-What-is-the-Märchen-Engine.md), lightly edited
- *Scope:* 4–5 pages
- **Content:**
  - What makes this system different (joy, kindness, integrity; morality as playable)
  - Core assumptions (narration-first, meaningful failure, character growth)
  - Iconography & notation (⚅ die, ⏳ time scales, ± degrees, σ composite)
  - Communication and table culture (Stay in Communication, Have Fun, Value Others' Time)
- **Key Decisions:** Tone is set here. Warm, inclusive, uncompromising on table health.

#### **Chapter 2: Character Creation**
- *Source:* Extract & formalize from intro + subsystem references (scattered across WIP)
- *Scope:* 8–10 pages
- **Content:**
  - Character concept & narrative arc
  - **Ability Scores** (Cunning, Perception, Deception, Empathy, Will, Fortitude, Physique, Reflexes, Mystique)
    - Distribution methods (roll 4D8 × 2, assign as desired; or point-buy)
    - Four human aspects: Physical, Mental, Social, Magical (the 4D8 lens)
  - **Moral Aspects** (playable character traits)
    - 12 dimensions: Care, Fairness, Loyalty, Authority, Sanctity, Liberty, Equity, Honesty, Hospitality, Wisdom, Humility, Self-Control
    - Selection or rolling method (weighted?)
    - How moral choices affect play (introduced briefly; full mechanics in Part II)
  - **Tradition Selection** (Martial, Magical Schools, or Mundane paths)
  - **Derived Stats** (Health, Composure, Engagement Defense, etc.)
  - **Starting Gear & Resources**
  - **Example Character:** Worked walkthrough from blank sheet to complete character
- **Key Decision:** Character creation is the **blockcap for publication.** Without this, readers can't sit down and play. This chapter unblocks all other work.

#### **Chapter 3: Core Resolution System (4D8 Task Resolution)**
- *Source:* Game Rules for a 4D8 System.md
- *Scope:* 6–8 pages
- **Content:**
  - When to roll (and when to narrate)
  - Rolling the dice: four-die pools, modifiers, degree of success
  - Difficulty target (Ease levels: Routine 12, Standard 18, Hard 24, Epic 30, Legendary 36)
  - Interpretation (total vs. degree; 0–3 = fail, 4–6 = success, 7–9 = strong success, 10+ = exceptional)
  - Four Aspects applied (Physical, Mental, Social, Magical) with examples
  - Partial success & failure as narrative opportunity
  - **Worked Example:** Full resolution cycle (player describes action → set difficulty → roll 4D8 + modifiers → interpret degree → narrate outcome)
- **Key Decision:** This is the mechanical heart. Must be crystal clear, not abstract.

### **PART II: CORE PLAY (The Three Pillars)**

**Reading Goal:** Master the three major modes of conflict & interaction.

#### **Chapter 4: Physical Conflict & Combat**
- *Scope:* 10–12 pages
- **Content:**
  - When combat begins (vs. roleplay, vs. engagement)
  - Initiative (draw from relevant ability or flat order)
  - Turn structure (action, movement, reaction; simplified to serve narrative)
  - Attack & defense (4D8 rolls, armor modifiers, degree = damage)
  - Special maneuvers (disarm, grapple, called shots) with mechanical anchor
  - Damage & healing (Health stat, injury consequences, poisons/conditions)
  - **Integration Note:** How to transition between Social Engagement and physical combat mid-scene
  - **Worked Example:** Party ambush scene (3–4 rounds) with multiple participants
  - When combat ends (surrender, retreat, reconciliation)

#### **Chapter 5: Social Conflict & Engagement**
- *Source:* Social Combat.md, restructured as "Engagement"
- *Scope:* 10–12 pages
- **Content:**
  - Engagement vs. roleplay (when mechanics matter)
  - Participants & stakes (who's trying to convince/charm/intimidate whom, and what's at risk)
  - Engagement Defense (derived stat; based on Perception + Composure)
  - Three Exchange types: **Influence** (convince), **Composure** (resist emotional pressure), **Cunning** (deceive/maneuver)
  - Exchange resolution (single 4D8 roll per participant; degree = advantage in next exchange)
  - Escalation & de-escalation (when does Engagement end? Can it become physical violence?)
  - **Moral Aspects in Engagement:** How honesty, empathy, and loyalty affect difficulty/degree
  - **Worked Example:** Negotiation with patron (3–4 exchanges), including a bluff and a sincere appeal
  - Engagement in campaign context (faction reputation, NPC relationships)

#### **Chapter 6: Magic & Magical Schools (Three Realms)**
- *Source:* New Magical Schools.md + game mechanics bridge (currently missing from WIP)
- *Scope:* 14–16 pages
- **Content:**
  - **Magic Philosophy:** Why magic exists in Märchen (narrative power, not optimization loophole)
  - **Three Realms:** Physical, Mental, Spiritual (and how they intersect)
  - **Magical Attributes:** Potency (casting power), Channeling (resilience/connection), [Third attribute TBD]
  - **Casting Framework** (currently MISSING — highest priority):
    - How to cast a spell: choose school/level → set difficulty (based on effect scope) → roll 4D8 + Potency/Channeling → resolve degree
    - Example spell resolution (simple & complex)
    - Resource cost: mana, fatigue, emotional toll, or component scarcity
    - Time to cast (Atoms, Moments, Rounds)
    - Abuse prevention (cooldowns, side effects, narrative cost)
  - **Nine Magical Schools:** (one per realm × three tiers)
    - Entropy (decay, endings), Elementalism (forces & matter), Arcane (knowledge, binding)
    - Metaphysics (mind, dreams, perception), Transmutation (change, growth), Communion (connection, harmony)
    - Sanctification (sacred, protection), Sacrifice (bargains, consequences), [Ninth school TBD]
    - For each school: 3 signature spells (levels 1–3) with flavor & mechanics
  - **Learning Magic:** Character creation choices vs. mid-campaign discovery
  - **Integration:** How magic interacts with combat, engagement, and morality
  - **Worked Example:** Mage casts a signature Metaphysics spell (read surface thoughts); encounter consequences

### **PART III: SYSTEMS & DEPTH (The Rulebook Layers)**

**Reading Goal:** Add texture and consequence to campaigns.

#### **Chapter 7: Moral Aspects & Playable Morality**
- *Source:* Morality Aspects.md, with mechanical grounding (currently philosophical only)
- *Scope:* 8–10 pages
- **Content:**
  - **Recap:** The 12 moral dimensions from Chapter 2
  - **Mechanical Options** (choose one approach, or hybrid):
    - **Option A (Trait-Based):** High/low aspects unlock special abilities and roleplay expectations
    - **Option B (Situational):** Acting against moral core imposes difficulty penalty or save requirement
    - **Option C (Social Capital):** Honesty/Fairness affect Engagement difficulty; Loyalty builds faction support
  - **Advancement:** Can moral aspects shift during play? How? (Yes, through significant choices)
  - **Consequence Framework:** What happens when a PC betrays their own values
  - **Examples:** Party debate on ransom vs. execution; NPC emotional manipulation; faction-scale moral choice
  - **Referee Guidance:** How to adjudicate moral conflicts without railroad or ambiguity

#### **Chapter 8: Nemesis & Legacy of Scars (Meaningful Opposition)**
- *Source:* A-Legacy-of-Scars-The-Märchen-System.md
- *Scope:* 8–10 pages
- **Content:**
  - What is a Nemesis (memorable foe who grows across campaign)
  - **Rank System:** Ranks 1–8, earned through confrontations (not just levels)
  - **FAME Framework:** Fitness (physical threat), Academic (cunning), Mystical (magical), Emotional (psychological)
  - Character creation: introducing the nemesis (what did they do to earn enmity?)
  - Escalation: how nemesis rank increases, what that means for encounters
  - Redemption or destruction: endings for nemesis arcs
  - **Quick Reference:** Nemesis stat block template
  - **Worked Example:** 6-session nemesis arc (introduction → rank escalation → climactic confrontation)

#### **Chapter 9: Magic Items & Named Artifacts**
- *Source:* Magic-Items-Rules.md (exemplary; minimal changes needed)
- *Scope:* 10–12 pages
- **Content:**
  - **Item Philosophy:** Why named items matter (story threads, character investment, not power-scaling)
  - **Bond System:** Items grow in power through narrative connection (Ranks 1–8, tied to character FAME)
  - **Item Creation:** GM-defined or player-discovered, with mechanical template
  - **Properties & Abilities:** What a rank-3 item can do vs. rank-8 (scope matters)
  - **Integration:** Named items as campaign threads
  - **Märchen Compatibility Scoring:** Rubric for adapting items from other systems
  - **Full Example Item Sheet:** One artifact with ranks 1–8 detailed (ranks 4–8 sketched)

#### **Chapter 10: Bastions & Downtime (Anchors & Advancement)**
- *Source:* Bastion Rules Cleaned up.md, heavily rewritten to feel Märchen-native
- *Scope:* 12–14 pages
- **Content:**
  - **Sanctuary (instead of "Bastion"):** A safe place, faction seat, or magical refuge
  - **Seasonal Turns:** Pacing downtime as chapters of a story (4 turns = 1 year)
  - **Sanctuaries & Resources:** Facilities (e.g., Library, Forge, Ritual Circle, Infirmary) generate narrative & mechanical benefits
  - **Moral Dimension:** How Sanctuary is founded & maintained (oath, kindness, or darker pacts?) affects loyalty and power
  - **Activities During Downtime:** Training, crafting, faction engagement, personal story arcs
  - **Integration with Nemesis, Magical Items, Engagement:** Sanctuary is where long-term consequences play out
  - **3–4 Worked Examples:** Using Sanctuary in a campaign
  - **Optional Module:** Can skip Sanctuary entirely for campaign mobility (quick-reference variant)

### **PART IV: RUNNING THE GAME (The Referee's Craft)**

**Reading Goal:** Give GMs confidence to improvise, adjudicate, and stay true to Märchen tone.

#### **Chapter 11: Game Mastering the Märchen Engine**
- *Scope:* 10–12 pages
- **Content:**
  - **Core GM Principles:** Stay true to the intro (joy, kindness, integrity—even when NPCs oppose PCs)
  - **Adjudication:** How to handle edge cases, rule ambiguities, and player creative nonsense
  - **NPC Design:** Quick stat blocks, moral profiles (how does this NPC resolve conflict?)
  - **Setting Tone:** Märchen flavor comes from fairy tale logic (curses have costs, deals matter, heroes earn happy endings through choice)
  - **Difficult Conversations:** When table expectations clash
  - **House Rules & Variants:** How to modify Märchen Engine responsibly
  - **Prep & Improvisation:** Balancing structure with spontaneity

#### **Chapter 12: Adventure Structure & Campaigns**
- *Source:* Adventure Structure.md (currently skeletal), rewritten with Märchen-specific examples
- *Scope:* 12–14 pages
- **Content:**
  - **Campaign Arcs vs. Episodic Play:** Märchen works for both; this chapter assumes arc-based campaigns (3–6 arcs, 6–12 sessions per arc)
  - **Arc Structure:**
    - Prologue: Moral question or NPC in distress (establishes stakes)
    - Rising Action: 2–3 confrontations (combat, engagement, or investigation)
    - Nemesis Escalation: Introduce or advance nemesis rank
    - Climax: Major choice or battle (consequences matter)
    - Downtime/Resolution: Sanctuary activities, moral reflection, Named Item discovery
  - **Pacing & Seasonal Activities:** Optional downtime module (from WIP) for campaign rhythm
  - **Multi-Arc Campaigns:** How to chain 2–3 arcs into cohesive story
  - **Worked Example: 12-Session Campaign Arc** (CRITICAL — currently missing)
    - Session 1: Character creation + first moral choice
    - Sessions 2–4: First arc (Nemesis Rank 1 revealed)
    - Sessions 5–6: Sanctuary downtime + Named Item discovery
    - Sessions 7–10: Second arc (Nemesis Rank 2–3, social engagement subplot)
    - Sessions 11–12: Climax + epilogue
    - Breakdown: when GMs introduce subsystems, how they pace encounters
  - **Campaign Variants:** Intrigue-focused (more Engagement), dungeon-crawl style, wilderness exploration

## EDITORIAL RISKS & MITIGATION

### **Risk 1: Casting Framework Missing (CRITICAL)**
- **Problem:** Chapter 6 (Magic) references spell mechanics that don't exist in WIP. Readers can't adjudicate casting.
- **Impact:** Core book feels incomplete; magic is unsalvageable from WIP without new work.
- **Mitigation:** **Before finalizing Part III, write "How to Cast a Spell" section with:**
  - 3–5 worked examples (simple spell, complex spell, abuse case)
  - Resource & difficulty matrix
  - Integration with 4D8 core resolution
  - Time cost clarification
  - **ETA:** 2–3 hours. This is blockcap for core book publication.

### **Risk 2: Character Creation Bridge Missing**
- **Problem:** Chapter 2 must be self-contained and complete. Currently scattered across WIP.
- **Impact:** Readers can't make characters; system feels unfinished.
- **Mitigation:** **Extract & consolidate:**
  - Ability names, ranges, and distribution methods (from intro, 4D8 rules, subsystems)
  - Moral Aspects mechanical options (currently philosophical in WIP)
  - Tradition selection & starting resources
  - Add worked example (blank sheet → complete character)
  - **ETA:** 3–4 hours. Critical path item.

### **Risk 3: Campaign Example Lacking**
- **Problem:** Chapters 11–12 describe running campaigns, but no 6–12 session worked example exists.
- **Impact:** GMs don't know how subsystems (Nemesis, Sanctuary, Moral Aspects) interlock; they'll feel optional.
- **Mitigation:** **Write one integrated campaign arc:**
  - Show character creation → first moral choice → Nemesis introduction → Engagement subplot → Sanctuary activity → Nemesis escalation → climax
  - ~20–30 page worked example showing all systems in play
  - This is the **proof of concept** that Märchen Engine is coherent.
  - **ETA:** 4–6 hours. High marketing value; unlocks subsequent supplement confidence.

### **Risk 4: Bastion Rules Feel D&D-Adjacent**
- **Problem:** WIP file (1,027 lines) appears adapted from D&D 5e Tasha's. Readers may doubt originality.
- **Impact:** Sanctuary (Chapter 10) reads as "D&D with prettier intro." Trust erodes.
- **Mitigation:** **Rewrite Sanctuary rules to:**
  - Use Märchen terminology (Sanctuary, not Bastion; Patron, not Faction; Seasonal Turns, not Downtime)
  - Anchor in Three Realms (does the Sanctuary tap Physical realm magic? Mental research? Spiritual protection?)
  - Add moral dimension (sanctuary built on betrayal generates power but attracts enemies)
  - Include 2–3 worked examples of Sanctuary in campaign context (showing integration with Nemesis & Named Items)
  - **ETA:** 4–5 hours. This chapter must feel native to Märchen.

### **Risk 5: Morality Feeling Philosophical, Not Mechanical**
- **Problem:** Chapter 7 (Moral Aspects) is beautifully written philosophy. It must become playable.
- **Impact:** Readers admire the values but don't know when/how they matter at table.
- **Mitigation:** **Add "Mechanical Options" section:**
  - Option A: Aspects as character traits unlocking abilities
  - Option B: Aspects as situational penalties/bonuses
  - Option C: Aspects as social capital in Engagement
  - Include worked examples showing each option in play
  - **ETA:** 2–3 hours. Moderate priority.

### **Risk 6: Magical Schools Lack Integration with Combat/Engagement**
- **Problem:** Chapter 6 covers schools in isolation. No examples showing magic used in combat or social conflict.
- **Impact:** Readers don't know when/how mages use magic; spells feel like separate subsystem.
- **Mitigation:** **In Chapter 6, add integration sidebars:**
  - "Using Magic in Combat" (mage casts while allies fight)
  - "Using Magic in Engagement" (charm, intimidate via magical effect)
  - "Using Magic in Downtime" (learning, crafting, faction work)
  - Include worked example scenario per sidebar.
  - **ETA:** 2–3 hours. Can be polished during editorial pass.

### **Risk 7: Terminology Inconsistency**
- **Problem:** WIP mixes "Social Combat" / "Engagement," "Bastion" / "Sanctuary," inconsistent ability names, Märchen/Marchen spelling.
- **Impact:** Readers doubt professionalism; have to re-read to understand.
- **Mitigation:** **Before typesetting, standardize:**
  - Terminology: Engagement (always), Sanctuary (always), all ability names (core stat block in Chapter 2)
  - Spelling: "Märchen Engine" (canonical; use umlaut throughout)
  - Narrator role: decide on "Referee," "Game Master," or "Narrator" and lock it in
  - Voice: consistent second-person address (advice) or third-person reference (rules)
  - Run a stylesheet + spot-check all chapters.
  - **ETA:** 2–3 hours during editorial pass. Low complexity; high impact.

### **Risk 8: Advanced Subsystems Bloating Core Book**
- **Problem:** Natural Weapons, Levels of Knowledge, Escalation/De-escalation are valuable but not core.
- **Impact:** Core book becomes 400+ pages; publication delayed; learners overwhelmed.
- **Mitigation:** **Clear decision:**
  - Core book: 200–240 pages (Parts I–IV + Appendices A–F)
  - Natural Weapons → future Gear supplement
  - Levels of Knowledge → formalize separately or archive
  - Escalation/De-escalation → include as Chapter 12 variant (one page sidebar)
  - Set a hard page limit and stick to it.
  - **ETA:** Decision now; saves weeks later.

## FINAL DECISION: SCOPE & COMMITMENT

**As Product Owner, I'm locking in the following:**

1. **Core Book Scope:** Parts I–IV + Appendices A–F = ~240 pages (target; not hard limit).
2. **Blockcap Items:**
   - Casting Framework (Risk 1) — must exist before ship
   - Character Creation consolidation (Risk 2) — must exist before ship
   - One 12-session campaign example (Risk 3) — must exist before ship
3. **Editorial Standards:**
   - All chapters pass the "Could a GM run this?" test before going to print
   - Terminology consistent throughout (style guide enforced)
   - All subsystems integrated into campaign example (no islands)
4. **Publication Plan:**
   - Core book ships standalone (complete, playable system)
   - Supplements (Light of Life campaign, Small-Alpine-Mirror setting, Advanced Options) follow 6–12 months later
   - Each supplement builds on core; nothing in core assumes supplements
5. **Success Criteria:**
   - A new player can read Chapter 2 and make a character
   - A new GM can read Chapters 11–12 and run a 6-session campaign
   - A reader finish the core book with no missing mechanical questions
   - System feels cohesive, not modular (Märchen DNA evident in every chapter)

title: Outcome Bias vs Narrative Fallacy
slug: outcome-bias-vs-narrative-fallacy
left_slug: narrative-errors
right_slug: narrative-errors
main_difference: Outcome Bias judges the quality of the decision by how things ended, while Narrative Fallacy builds a tidy story that explains the ending too neatly.
left_summary: Outcome Bias asks, often unfairly, whether the choice was good or bad by looking at the result alone. It confuses luck with judgment.
right_summary: Narrative Fallacy turns a messy chain of causes into a clean, memorable explanation. It confuses a satisfying story with an accurate one.
confusion_example: After a risky product launch succeeds, a team calls the original decision brilliant because the ending was good. That is Outcome Bias because the reasoning is being graded by the result. If the same team then tells a neat story that one heroic choice caused the whole success while ignoring luck, timing, and market conditions, that is Narrative Fallacy because the messy reality has been compressed into a clean arc.
memory_trick: Outcome Bias says, "The ending proves the decision." Narrative Fallacy says, "The tidy story explains the ending."

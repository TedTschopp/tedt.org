---
title: Biases vs Heuristics
slug: biases-vs-heuristics
left_slug: cognitive-biases
right_slug: heuristics
main_difference: A bias is the tilt in judgment, while a heuristic is the quick shortcut that may create that tilt.
left_summary: Biases push judgment in a direction. They can make a person overtrust the first number, favorite evidence, or recent story.
right_summary: Heuristics are fast mental rules that save time. They are useful in many daily choices, but they can mislead when the problem is complex.
confusion_example: A parent hears one scary story about a product and now thinks the product is common and risky. The shortcut is the availability heuristic. The tilted judgment that follows is a bias in how risk gets judged.
memory_trick: Heuristic means the fast tool. Bias means the bend it can leave behind.

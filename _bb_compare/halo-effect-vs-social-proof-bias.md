title: Halo Effect vs Social Proof Bias

main_difference: Halo Effect lets one admired trait shape your judgment, while Social Proof Bias lets other people's behavior shape your judgment.
left_summary: Halo Effect starts with a glow around one person, brand, or idea. Confidence, beauty, charm, or polish spills over into unrelated judgments.
right_summary: Social Proof Bias starts with the crowd. The visible behavior of other people gets treated like a shortcut for safety, truth, or quality.
confusion_example: A polished founder makes a weak idea feel stronger. That is Halo Effect because one strong personal trait colors everything else. If the same idea seems right because many people are already backing it, that is Social Proof Bias because the crowd is doing the work.
memory_trick: Halo follows the star. Social proof follows the crowd.

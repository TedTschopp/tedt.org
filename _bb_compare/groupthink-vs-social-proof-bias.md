title: Groupthink vs Social Proof Bias
slug: groupthink-vs-social-proof-bias
left_slug: decision-traps
right_slug: social-perception-errors
main_difference: Groupthink is a group decision process that suppresses dissent, while Social Proof Bias is a shortcut where other people's behavior feels like evidence.
left_summary: Groupthink happens inside a group that wants unity so badly that honest challenge fades out. Silence, pressure, and harmony distort the decision.
right_summary: Social Proof Bias can happen in or outside a group. It uses visible behavior from other people as a clue for what is right, safe, or worth doing.
confusion_example: A team stays quiet during a risky meeting because nobody wants to be the lone critic. That is Groupthink because dissent is being suppressed inside the room. If someone joins a choice mainly because many other people already seem to support it, that is Social Proof Bias because the crowd itself is acting like proof.
memory_trick: Groupthink protects harmony inside the room. Social proof copies visible behavior.

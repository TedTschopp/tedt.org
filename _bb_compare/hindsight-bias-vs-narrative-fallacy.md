title: Hindsight Bias vs Narrative Fallacy
slug: hindsight-bias-vs-narrative-fallacy
left_slug: narrative-errors
right_slug: narrative-errors
main_difference: Hindsight Bias makes the outcome feel obvious after it happens, while Narrative Fallacy turns messy events into a neat story that feels more explanatory than it really is.
left_summary: Hindsight Bias rewrites memory after the ending is known. It shrinks how uncertain the event really felt at the time.
right_summary: Narrative Fallacy builds a satisfying story with clean causes and a tidy lesson. It smooths away complexity, luck, and noise.
confusion_example: After a launch fails, a team says the failure was obvious from the start. That is Hindsight Bias because the known ending is rewriting how predictable it felt. If the team then tells a neat story that one leadership mistake caused everything, that is Narrative Fallacy because complexity has been compressed into a clean arc.
memory_trick: Hindsight says, “We should have seen it.” Narrative Fallacy says, “Here is the tidy story of why it happened.”

---
title: Projection Bias vs False Consensus Effect
slug: projection-bias-vs-false-consensus-effect
left_slug: cognitive-biases
right_slug: cognitive-biases
main_difference: Projection Bias assumes another person thinks or feels like you do, while False Consensus Effect assumes lots of people probably agree with you.
left_summary: Projection Bias uses your own current motives, preferences, or emotional state as a shortcut for another person's inner world.
right_summary: False Consensus Effect uses your own viewpoint and your immediate circle as a shortcut for how common that viewpoint must be in the broader population.
confusion_example: A manager loves speed and assumes one specific teammate also wants a fast decision. That is Projection Bias because one person's mind is being modeled after the manager's. If the manager then says most employees probably want the same thing, that becomes False Consensus Effect because the claim expands from one mind to the whole crowd.
memory_trick: Projection Bias says, "You are probably like me." False Consensus Effect says, "Most people are probably like me."

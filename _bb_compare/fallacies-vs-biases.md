---
title: Fallacies vs Biases
slug: fallacies-vs-biases
left_slug: logical-fallacies
right_slug: cognitive-biases
main_difference: A fallacy is a broken move in the argument, while a bias is a mental tilt in how someone judges the facts.
left_summary: Fallacies live in the reasoning you can point to. You can quote the line, test it, and show where it fails.
right_summary: Biases live in how the mind notices, favors, or filters information. They shape what feels true before the person even argues it.
confusion_example: A manager says, “Everybody supports this plan, so it must be right.” The crowd part is a fallacy in the argument, but the same manager may also have confirmation bias if they only notice support and ignore warnings.
memory_trick: If the bug is in the sentence, think fallacy. If the bug is in the mental tilt behind the sentence, think bias.

title: Overconfidence vs Illusion of Explanatory Depth
slug: overconfidence-vs-illusion-of-explanatory-depth
left_slug: metacognitive-illusions
right_slug: metacognitive-illusions
main_difference: Overconfidence is being too sure overall, while the Illusion of Explanatory Depth is feeling sure you understand the mechanism when you do not.
left_summary: Overconfidence shows up as certainty that runs ahead of evidence, skill, or track record. The person feels more accurate than they really are.
right_summary: Illusion of Explanatory Depth shows up when something feels understood until a step-by-step explanation is required. The weakness is in the supposed depth of understanding.
confusion_example: A leader says a plan will definitely work even though the evidence is thin. That is Overconfidence. If the same leader says the system is simple but cannot explain how it actually works, that is the Illusion of Explanatory Depth.
memory_trick: Overconfidence says, “I am sure.” Explanatory-depth illusion says, “I understand,” until details arrive.

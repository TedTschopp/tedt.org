Quote 1

Ignore her idea. She always wants attention.

Show answer

Attacking the Person.

The line attacks the speaker instead of answering the idea.

Quote 2

So you want us to cancel every rule and let chaos take over.

Show answer

Straw Man.

The point was twisted into a weaker and more extreme version.

Quote 3

Everybody online believes it, so it must be true.

Show answer

Bandwagon Fallacy.

The crowd is being used as proof instead of evidence.

Quote 4

I already spent so much on this that I have to keep going.

Show answer

Sunk Cost Fallacy.

Past cost is being used to justify a bad future choice.

Quote 5

I can remember one scary case, so this must happen all the time.

Show answer

Availability Heuristic.

The easy-to-recall example is being mistaken for the broader pattern.

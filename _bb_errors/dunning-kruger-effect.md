one_line_definition: Limited skill or knowledge can make people overrate how well they understand or perform.
plain_english: >-
  The Dunning-Kruger Effect shows up when a little knowledge creates too much confidence. A person learns the basic terms, gets one success, or sees one simple explanation, and suddenly feels far more expert than the evidence supports. This does not mean beginners are foolish. It means that when you know only the surface, you often do not yet see what you are missing. Real depth usually brings more humility, because the hidden complexity becomes visible. A good check is whether confidence rose faster than tested skill.

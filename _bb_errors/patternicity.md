one_line_definition: Finding a meaningful pattern, signal, or design in data or events that may mostly be random noise.
plain_english: >-
  Patternicity happens when the mind links dots too fast. People notice a streak, a coincidence, a cluster, or a repeated symbol and start treating it like proof that something meaningful is going on. Sometimes patterns are real. The bug is jumping from "I notice a shape" to "This must reveal a hidden cause, message, or plan" without enough evidence. The mind is built to detect patterns because that often helps. But it can also turn randomness into stories. A better move is to ask whether the pattern is strong, repeatable, and tested, or whether it only feels striking.

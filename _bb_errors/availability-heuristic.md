one_line_definition: Judging how common or likely something is by how easily examples come to mind.
plain_english: >-
  Availability Heuristic is a fast mental shortcut. If an example comes to mind quickly, the mind treats it as common, important, or likely. That can help in some situations. But vivid stories, recent news, and dramatic events are easier to remember than ordinary reality. So the shortcut can badly distort risk and frequency. One scary story can outweigh a hundred boring facts. The fix is simple: do not confuse easy recall with good measurement.

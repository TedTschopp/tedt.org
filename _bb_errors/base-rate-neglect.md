one_line_definition: Ignoring the broad background odds and focusing too much on a vivid detail or test result.
plain_english: >-
  Base Rate Neglect happens when people ignore the big background numbers and focus on a more vivid clue. A test result, one trait, or one story can feel very persuasive. But if the underlying rate is low or high, that background matters a lot. Without it, people wildly overestimate how special the case is. This bug often appears in health fears, hiring judgments, and crime stories. To reason well, start with the base rate before adding the eye-catching detail.

one_line_definition: Mistaking the ease or smoothness of reading, hearing, or recalling something for proof that it is true or well learned.
plain_english: >-
  Fluency Illusion appears when ease becomes fake evidence. If an explanation sounds smooth, a sentence is catchy, or a page is easy to scan, the mind starts treating that ease like proof. People may feel they understand the material, trust the claim, or assume they will remember it later simply because it went down easily. But smooth is not the same as solid. Some truths are hard at first, and some polished claims are weak. A better check is to test recall, explanation, and evidence instead of trusting the nice feeling of flow.

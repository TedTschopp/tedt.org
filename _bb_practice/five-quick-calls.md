title: Five Quick Calls
slug: five-quick-calls
instructions: Read each short scene, choose the best match, and then check the feedback.
questions:
  - prompt: A student says, “Ignore Maya’s point about the book. She is always trying to sound smart.”
    options:
      - Attacking the Person
      - Confirmation Bias
      - Sunk Cost Fallacy
    answer_index: 0
    explanation: This attacks Maya instead of answering her point. That is Attacking the Person, also called Ad Hominem.
    link: /tools/Brain-Bugs/errors/ad-hominem/
  - prompt: A teammate says, “So you want us to delete every rule and let people do anything they want.” That was not what the original speaker said.
    options:
      - Straw Man
      - Bandwagon Fallacy
      - Black-and-White Thinking
    answer_index: 0
    explanation: The point was rewritten into a weaker, easier target. That is Straw Man.
    link: /tools/Brain-Bugs/errors/straw-man/
  - prompt: A buyer keeps paying for a gym app they never use because they already paid for six months.
    options:
      - Confirmation Bias
      - Sunk Cost Fallacy
      - Bandwagon Fallacy
    answer_index: 1
    explanation: Past cost is being treated like a reason to keep losing value. That is Sunk Cost Fallacy.
    link: /tools/Brain-Bugs/errors/sunk-cost-fallacy/
  - prompt: A friend only saves posts that support their favorite diet and scrolls past the studies that challenge it.
    options:
      - Confirmation Bias
      - Straw Man
      - Black-and-White Thinking
    answer_index: 0
    explanation: They are favoring evidence that fits the belief they already want. That is Confirmation Bias.
    link: /tools/Brain-Bugs/errors/confirmation-bias/
  - prompt: A coach says, “Either you are fully committed or you are a total failure.”
    options:
      - Bandwagon Fallacy
      - Black-and-White Thinking
      - Ad Hominem
    answer_index: 1
    explanation: The middle ground disappears and only extremes remain. That is Black-and-White Thinking.
    link: /tools/Brain-Bugs/errors/black-and-white-thinking/

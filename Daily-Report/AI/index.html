AI Search Agents Mostly Confirm What They Already Know

Researchers at Harbin Institute of Technology tested leading AI search agents — including GPT-5.4 and Kimi K2.6 — on a time-based benchmark designed to require genuine web research. The result: these agents overwhelmingly use web access to validate their training-set assumptions rather than discover new information.

Why it matters: Enterprises deploying AI for competitive intelligence, market research, or regulatory monitoring are at risk if their agents are quietly self-confirming instead of surfacing fresh data. The benchmark — designed to reveal this behavior — should serve as a wake-up call for any team that assumes "web-enabled AI" equals "up-to-date AI."

  • What should I do as a leader? Require human verification steps for AI-generated research outputs, especially for time-sensitive decisions. Treat AI search as a first draft, not a final source.
  • What should I do? When using AI search tools for research, manually check that the sources cited are actually new — not just familiar pages the model has seen before.

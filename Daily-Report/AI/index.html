Research Alert: LLMs Silently Corrupt Documents When Given Delegated Tasks

A new paper on arXiv titled "LLMs Corrupt Your Documents When You Delegate" presents findings that should concern any enterprise using AI to draft, revise, or summarize contracts, policies, regulatory filings, or compliance documents. The research demonstrates that when large language models are given delegated editing tasks — rewrite this section, summarize this clause, update this paragraph — they introduce subtle but systematic changes to meaning, scope, and accuracy that do not read as errors on casual review. The corruptions are not random; they reflect the model's training biases about what a document "should" say.

What should I do as a leader? Establish a mandatory human review gate for any AI-drafted or AI-revised document that carries legal, regulatory, or contractual weight. Do not treat AI-generated text as reviewed text — they are different categories.

What should I do? Before submitting any document that an AI tool touched, read it against the original source or prior version line by line. Compressed summaries and restructured paragraphs are the highest-risk areas for undetected changes.

Customer-Back Engineering: Why 70% of Digital Value Is Never Captured

A separate MIT Technology Review analysis, citing McKinsey research, argues that organizations capture less than one-third of the value expected from digital (and AI) investments. The root cause: most enterprises start with technological capabilities and find applications for them, rather than beginning with specific customer problems. The piece advocates for "customer-back engineering" — defining the outcome first, then selecting the technology.

Why it matters: This is a direct challenge to the common pattern of AI investment driven by vendor roadmaps and competitive pressure rather than diagnosed business problems. With AI budgets under increasing scrutiny, teams that cannot articulate customer or operational outcomes per dollar spent are increasingly vulnerable to cuts.

What should I do as a leader? For every AI initiative in flight, require a one-paragraph statement of the customer or operational problem it solves and how success is measured. Deprioritize those that cannot be written.

Security, Risk & Compliance

Meta's AI Agent Was Used to Hijack Instagram Accounts

An attack first reported by 404 Media showed that adversaries exploited Meta's AI customer support agent to take over Instagram accounts—including a dormant account once associated with the Obama White House. The method required no malware or zero-day exploit. The attackers simply told the agent to link target accounts to email addresses they controlled, and the agent complied.

This is a demonstration of an over-permissioned AI agent in the wild. The system had account modification authority and lacked the skepticism to detect a social engineering attempt. For any enterprise operating customer-facing AI agents with write or modify permissions—account changes, order adjustments, credential resets—this incident is a direct preview of how your AI will be tested by adversaries who have read the same news you have.

What should I do as a leader?

  • Inventory every AI agent that has write access to account or identity records. Require human-in-the-loop verification for any operation that modifies account ownership or credentials.
  • Add adversarial prompt testing to your AI agent validation process before any public deployment.

What should I do?

  • If you configure or manage AI agents at your organization, ask: what is the worst thing an unauthorized user could ask our agent to do, and what currently stops it?

For enterprise AI deployments, the governance question is not only "does this AI work well?" but "what does it optimize for, and who decided?"

What should I do as a leader?

Anthropic: Claude Writes More Than 80% of Its Own Code & Wants a Pause Button

Anthropic shared internal data revealing that more than 80% of its production code now comes from Claude, and that its engineers are shipping eight times as much code per day as they were in 2024. In the same post, the company called for a global "AI pause button"—a mechanism that could slow or halt AI development if systems begin to improve themselves in ways that trigger safety concerns.

The combination is deliberately uncomfortable. Anthropic is publishing its own velocity as proof of what AI-assisted development can achieve while simultaneously asking whether humanity should have the ability to stop it. For enterprise engineering leaders, the 8x productivity figure is the most immediately actionable data point in the industry right now. It is a benchmark worth testing against your own teams.

The pause button argument will be easier to dismiss as philosophy. It should not be. As AI systems contribute more to their own development, the window between "AI accelerating human engineers" and "AI improving AI with minimal human review" narrows. Organizations that build governance frameworks now are better positioned than those who wait for the window to close.

What should I do as a leader?

  • Set a baseline today for what percentage of your engineering output involves AI-generated code. Measure it quarterly. Assess whether your current code review practices are adequate for AI-generated code at scale.

AI Gets "Loopy": Agent Swarms That Don't Stop

TechCrunch reports on a significant architectural shift in enterprise AI: "loops," where swarms of AI agents work continuously in the background with standing authorization to observe, decide, and act indefinitely. Unlike traditional agentic workflows that complete and return control, loop architectures are designed to run without a natural stop point.

Why it matters: Most enterprise AI governance policies were written for bounded, task-specific AI. Loop architectures represent a largely ungoverned new category where AI systems have ongoing authorization to modify data, initiate actions, and make decisions without human review intervals. Organizations deploying or purchasing software that contains loop-based agents need to define autonomy limits before incidents, not after.

  • What should I do as a leader? Convene AI governance, legal, and security teams to explicitly address agent autonomy scope in your AI policy. Define what systems, data, and actions AI agents may access without human approval.
  • What should I do? When evaluating agentic AI tools, ask vendors whether their system uses loop architectures and what scope-limiting or human-override controls exist.

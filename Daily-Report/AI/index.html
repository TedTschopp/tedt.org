Google CEO Sundar Pichai is now describing links and sources as merely a "part" of search—not its foundation. Security, Risk & Compliance

AI Reconstructed Dead Pilots' Voices—and Forced the NTSB to React

Researchers used AI to reconstruct cockpit voice recordings from spectrogram images—essentially rebuilding audio from a visual representation of sound—and published their findings from National Transportation Safety Board docket records. The NTSB responded by temporarily blocking public access to its entire online docket system to assess the exposure.

The enterprise implication runs wider than aviation safety. If a spectrogram image posted in a public docket is enough to reconstruct a voice, organizations need to reconsider what constitutes sensitive audio data. Meeting recordings, call-center logs, and even compressed voicemail archives may contain latent voice-reconstruction potential that current data-governance policies do not address.

What should I do as a leader? Commission a review of your organization's voice-data retention and disposal policies. Ensure data governance frameworks explicitly classify audio recordings—and their visual representations—as sensitive personal information requiring deletion schedules.

What should I do? Be cautious about publishing any audio, spectrogram images, or voice-related metadata in public or semi-public repositories and bug trackers.

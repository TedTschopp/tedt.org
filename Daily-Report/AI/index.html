OpenAI Launches ChatGPT Lockdown Mode to Contain Prompt Injection

OpenAI introduced "Lockdown Mode" for ChatGPT — a new setting that disables web browsing, Deep Research, and Agent Mode. The stated goal is to cut off the final step in a prompt injection attack chain: exfiltration of sensitive data through those outbound capabilities. The Decoder noted that Lockdown Mode addresses symptoms rather than root cause — prompt injection remains an unsolved foundational problem — and TechCrunch confirmed that even with Lockdown Mode active, ChatGPT could still be susceptible to injections; the feature reduces the likelihood of data being shared, not the likelihood of being attacked.

Why it matters: Enterprise ChatGPT deployments in legal, finance, and HR settings frequently handle confidential data. Lockdown Mode provides a meaningful risk-reduction layer even if imperfect. The fact that OpenAI prioritized shipping this feature signals real pressure from enterprise customers who have experienced or anticipate prompt injection exploits.

What should I do as a leader? Work with your AI governance team to enable Lockdown Mode on ChatGPT deployments for any team handling regulated or sensitive data. Treat it as a layered control — not a complete solution — and pair it with user training on social engineering risks.

What should I do? If your organization uses ChatGPT for anything involving client data, contracts, or personnel information, ask your IT team when Lockdown Mode will be enabled in your deployment.

layout: bb-story
title: Myths & Legends
story_family: Story Source Guide
lead: Myths make pride, panic, false certainty, and simple cause-and-effect stories easy to see.
why_this_source: Old myths and legends are useful teaching tools because they make a big thinking mistake visible in one dramatic scene. The characters often move fast, trust signs too quickly, or hold onto a story that feels meaningful even when the evidence is thin.
notice_points:
  - Watch for heroes who feel sure before they have enough proof.
  - Notice when one vivid event gets treated like a law of nature.
  - Ask whether the story explains too much with one neat cause.
featured_errors:
  - overconfidence-effect
  - sunk-cost-fallacy
  - narrative-fallacy
  - hindsight-bias
related_comparisons:
  - overconfidence-vs-illusion-of-explanatory-depth
  - sunk-cost-vs-escalation-of-commitment
story_spotlights:
  - source: Icarus
    title: Flying too high feels easy until reality shows up.
    summary: Icarus ignores the limits built into the warning he was given. Confidence grows faster than care, so the risk stops feeling real.
    why_it_matters: This scene makes overconfidence memorable because the mistake is not lack of effort. It is too much trust in a shaky judgment.
    errors:
      - overconfidence-effect
      - planning-fallacy
  - source: The Odyssey
    title: A long journey can trap people into defending the path they already chose.
    summary: Odysseus and his crew face moments where pride, loyalty to past effort, and bad group calls keep trouble alive longer than it should.
    why_it_matters: Myths are good at showing how sunk costs and group pressure can keep a bad plan moving.
    errors:
      - sunk-cost-fallacy
      - groupthink
  - source: Greek origin stories
    title: A neat story can feel more satisfying than a careful explanation.
    summary: Many myths explain messy events with one simple tale about a god, a curse, or a single turning point.
    why_it_matters: That makes myths a strong place to teach narrative fallacy and the human urge to turn noise into meaning.
    errors:
      - narrative-fallacy
      - hindsight-bias

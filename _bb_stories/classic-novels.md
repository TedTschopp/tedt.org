title: Classic Novels
story_family: Story Source Guide
lead: Longer novels let Brain Bugs build slowly through status, habits, pride, and repeated bad choices.
why_this_source: Classic novels help because they give a thinking mistake time to grow. Instead of one sharp scene, readers can watch a weak judgment repeat, deepen, and shape relationships over time.
notice_points:
  - Watch how first impressions stay in control for too long.
  - Notice when people keep investing in a bad path because they already paid a cost.
  - Ask whether status or charm is being mistaken for truth.
featured_errors:
  - anchoring-bias
  - fundamental-attribution-error
  - halo-effect
  - sunk-cost-fallacy
related_comparisons:
  - fallacies-vs-biases
  - sunk-cost-vs-escalation-of-commitment
story_spotlights:
  - source: Pride and Prejudice
    title: First impressions become anchors.
    summary: Characters form quick judgments about each other, then keep reading later behavior through that first frame.
    why_it_matters: This is a clean example of anchoring, halo effects, and social judgment errors.
    errors:
      - anchoring-bias
      - halo-effect
      - fundamental-attribution-error
  - source: Moby-Dick
    title: The mission keeps going long after the cost becomes obvious.
    summary: Captain Ahab keeps pushing the voyage toward his obsession even as the price rises for everyone around him.
    why_it_matters: Few stories show sunk cost and overcommitment as clearly as this one.
    errors:
      - sunk-cost-fallacy
      - overconfidence-effect
  - source: A Christmas Carol
    title: People are more complex than a harsh label.
    summary: The story starts with a rigid, all-or-nothing judgment about what kind of person Scrooge is and whether change is possible.
    why_it_matters: That makes it useful for teaching black-and-white thinking and the danger of fixed moral stories.
    errors:
      - black-and-white-thinking
      - narrative-fallacy

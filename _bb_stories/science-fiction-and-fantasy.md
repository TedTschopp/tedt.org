title: Science Fiction & Fantasy
story_family: Story Source Guide
lead: Strange worlds make familiar thinking traps easier to notice because the pressure gets turned up and simplified.
why_this_source: Fantasy and science fiction help readers step back from daily arguments and notice the pattern itself. When the world is unusual, the reasoning mistake often becomes easier to see.
notice_points:
  - Watch for people who trust a frame or label more than the real situation.
  - Notice when fear makes one vivid danger feel bigger than it is.
  - Ask whether hidden systems are being explained with more confidence than the evidence supports.
featured_errors:
  - false-dilemma
  - availability-heuristic
  - illusion-of-explanatory-depth
  - loss-aversion
related_comparisons:
  - biases-vs-heuristics
  - overconfidence-vs-illusion-of-explanatory-depth
story_spotlights:
  - source: Frankenstein
    title: Fear of the vivid example can outrun careful judgment.
    summary: Once the creature becomes the center of fear, extreme possibilities start to dominate how people read danger, blame, and cause.
    why_it_matters: The novel helps learners see how one striking case can distort judgment.
    errors:
      - availability-heuristic
      - correlation-vs-causation
  - source: The Wizard of Oz
    title: A dramatic frame can hide what is really happening.
    summary: Characters are pushed into simple either-or stories until the truth behind the performance becomes visible.
    why_it_matters: This makes the book useful for false dilemma and illusion-of-explanatory-depth lessons.
    errors:
      - false-dilemma
      - illusion-of-explanatory-depth
  - source: Fantasy stories about powerful objects or systems
    title: People cling to what they already hold, even when it harms them.
    summary: In many fantasy tales, characters resist giving up a powerful object or role because the loss feels larger than the danger of keeping it.
    why_it_matters: This pattern helps explain loss aversion in a vivid way.
    errors:
      - loss-aversion
      - sunk-cost-fallacy

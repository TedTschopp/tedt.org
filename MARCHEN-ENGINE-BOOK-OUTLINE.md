## FIVE CRITICAL STRUCTURAL RISKS

### Risk 1: Magic System Without Mechanical Grounding
**If:** New Magical Schools chapter appears before Core Task Resolution or without casting framework examples.

**Outcome:** Readers see beautiful lore (Three Realms, school descriptions) but have no idea how to resolve a spell cast at the table. System feels broken.

**Mitigation:**
- Casting Framework (spell rolling, resource cost, time, abuse prevention) is mandatory before any school description.
- Every school description must include ≥1 worked example spell with full resolution.
- Chapter 5 must always appear *after* Chapter 3 (4D8 dependency).

---

### Risk 2: Character Creation Unpublished or Weak
**If:** Chargen is scattered across multiple files or incomplete; readers can't actually *make* a character.

**Outcome:** Hard stop. System is unplayable. Readers abandon before Chapter 3.

**Mitigation:**
- Chapter 2 must be comprehensive (ability scores, moral aspects, items, bastion, example character).
- It must be *second* in the book, after intro.
- Include full worked example (Elara walkthrough).

---

### Risk 3: Bastion Feels Like D&D Borrowing, Not Märchen Native
**If:** Bastion rules lack Märchen DNA (no moral aspect ties, no Three Realms integration, generic terminology).

**Outcome:** Readers think, "This is D&D with a prettier intro." Trust in originality erodes. System feels derivative.

**Mitigation:**
- Rename to "Sanctuary" (Märchen flavor).
- Add sidebar: "How Morality Affects Your Sanctuary" (tie to Chapter 2 moral aspects).
- Include worked examples showing Sanctuary threatened by Nemesis.
- If rewrite is too costly, defer to Appendix B and mark as "Advanced Rules."

---

### Risk 4: No Proof of Concept = Readers Doubt Interlock
**If:** Book has 10 subsystems (Engagement, Magic, Items, Nemesis, Bastion, etc.) but no example showing them working together.

**Outcome:** Readers wonder if subsystems actually cohere. They treat each as isolated rule module. They doubt campaign viability.

**Mitigation:**
- Chapter 13 (campaign example) is non-negotiable.
- Must show Engagement + Magic + Items + Nemesis + Bastion in a single, coherent 6–12 session arc.
- This is the *most important* chapter after character creation.

---

### Risk 5: Morality Becomes Decorative Flavor if Not Mechanically Woven In
**If:** Moral Aspects (Chapter 2 intro + Appendix A) are read *separately* from subsystems; mechanical hooks are vague.

**Outcome:** Readers admire philosophy but don't know where it lives in rules. Morality is flavor, not mechanics.

**Mitigation:**
- Introduce all 12 dimensions in Chapter 2 (chargen), but keep mechanics light ("select 3 core aspects").
- In Chapter 4 (Social Engagement) and Chapter 8 (Bastion), add sidebar notes: "How Honesty affects Engagement" or "How Care affects Sanctuary morale."
- Full framework (consequence gates, advancement) in Appendix A, clearly labeled optional.

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

A book structured per this outline achieves these outcomes:

1. **Chargen Completeness:** By end of Chapter 2, a new player can create a complete, playable character.
2. **Mechanical Clarity:** By end of Chapter 3, a new GM can understand 4D8 and run a simple task resolution.
3. **Subsystem Coherence:** By end of Chapter 7, a new GM can run a scene using ≥3 subsystems (e.g., Social Engagement + Magic + Nemesis) without confusion.
4. **System Integrity:** After Chapter 13 (campaign example), a new reader feels confident all subsystems work together, not in isolation.
5. **Speed-Read Path:** A reader can skim core chapters + Chapter 13 in 2–3 hours and understand the system well enough to play.
6. **Depth Preserved:** Readers seeking depth can explore Appendices without feeling critical content was held back.

## IA Recommendation for Ted

**Decision:** This outline treats the Märchen Engine as a **complete, single-volume core RPG** (not a modular toolkit). This choice implies:
- High effort (13–19 days of focused work)
- High confidence (proof-of-concept campaign guarantees system works)
- Strong market signal (coherent game line, not scattered subsystems)

**Alternative:** If you prefer a "modular toolkit" approach:
- Publish Tier 1 subsystems (Magic Items, Social Combat, Nemesis) individually with series metadata
- Defer chargen and campaign example
- Publish as "beta game line, grow as you go"
- Effort: 4–5 days per subsystem

**Recommended path:** Invest in the complete book outline. The Alpine Mirror campaign is already written; character creation is a synthesis of existing content. Casting Framework is the largest new piece. The payoff is a coherent, publishable system that readers trust is complete.
